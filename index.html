Rachel's Basic Webpage

My final project will hopefully be a redesigned online portfolio that I create from scratch. Here is a list of the things I need to include on my final webpage:

  • About Me
  • Resume
  • Work
  • Contact

I like hosting services such as WordPress and SquareSpace, but I want my portfolio to be completely customizable - me in a website. I also want to be able to say that I made it myself because coding is a skill that not a lot of people in my career field have. Here are a couple of examples of online portfolios I've found that appear to be manually coded. I like the design and organizational elements of each.

Alyssa Ramirez

Alyssa's portfolio includes all of the components that I would want to include in mine. Instead of being organized into one page, she uses tabs to display her resumé, work and contact information. The tabs even have drop-down menus when necessary. Overall, I think her portfolio is professional, easy to use and visually appealing.
Below is a quote that Alyssa keeps on her home page. I'm sure it inspires her and she hopes it will do the same for those who visit her site.

"Bring the best of your authentic self to every opportunity." - John Jantsch, author of Duct Tape Marketing

There's not much else to say about my portfolio and what I want it to be. The functionality and ease of navigation are a lot more important to me than design elements, though I do think personal branding is pertinent to my field. More than anything, I want to walk away from this class feeling like I've learned skills that will last me a lifetime.

Laura Kern

Laura's portfolio reads more like a blog, but it still has all of the essential components of a professional website. I don't like the design as much as Alyssa's, but I can appreciate the simplicity of her minimalist color scheme. The blocks of text are easy to read.
Below is a quote that I think would resonate with Laura and anyone who visits her site. It's from a well-known master of advertising and one of the people I look up to most.

"Curiosity about life, in all its aspects, I think, is still the secret of great creative people." – Leo Burnett
